Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Benares or Kashi, is one of the oldest living cities in the world and a major cultural hub in India. It is considered a holy city in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, and has been a cultural and religious center in North India for thousands of years.

Uttrakhand

Uttarakhand, often referred to as the “Land of the Gods,” has a rich cultural tapestry woven with elements of both Kumaoni and Garhwali traditions. The culture is a reflection of the state’s geographical and historical influences, which can be seen in its architectural marvels, traditional clothing, vibrant festivals, folk dances, music, cuisine, art, and craft.

Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as “God’s Own Country,” is a state in the southern part of India known for its distinctive culture and traditions that have developed over millennia. The culture of Kerala is a harmonious blend of various religions, communities, regional cultures, and language variations.

Assam

Assam, located in the northeastern part of India, is a state rich in culture and tradition, reflecting a blend of Indo-Burmese, Mongolian, and Aryan influences. The Assamese culture is a vibrant tapestry of various racial stocks such as Mongoloid, Indo-Burmese, Indo-Iranian, and Aryan, which have evolved through a long assimilative process.
